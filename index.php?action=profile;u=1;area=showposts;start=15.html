General Discussion / Re: How did you discover CS2d
« on: July 23, 2016, 21:47:31 »
Still a lot of off-topic, but I am too soft so I will tell you my story anyway. To punish you I have made it really long.

Back in 2008 I was looking for some free, simple, fun multiplayer game to play online. Since I am a Linux guy, I wanted to be able to play in Linux. After some searching I found this site with an extensive list of games. I started going through the list and tried a few ones. There wasn't any interesting, really. But then I found this cute looking game called Counter-Strike 2D. I had played Counter-Strike so it caught my eye immediately. I downloaded it and started playing. Wow! This was really fun! I played on a CTF server for the rest of the evening. I couldn't wait to get up in the morning to continue playing.

After a good night's sleep I continued exploring the server list. I joined a random server. It was running in construction mode, but I had no idea at first. What the heck were the other players doing with those wrenches? This wasn't part of the original Counter-Strike. Oh, they are building stuff. How do I do that? The lack of instructions was a slight problem but after some trial and error I succeeded. Cool, I can create stuff! Construction mode had been introduced recently so there were lots of construction servers out there. Many of them used the original DE and CS maps, which have no real objective in construction mode, except building and killing players from the other team. That didn't matter. Construction mode was more hyped than Pokémon Go at the time.

After a few weeks of getting my noob ass whipped I discovered the noble art of camping. Camping could really compensate for my lack of skills and it made the game more interesting. Waiting under a tree to ambush unexpecting players that came too close was really fun. For me. The victims were usually not that amused. One even referred to me as a "f*****g camper". Hmm, that sounded quite cool, I thought. If I replace the bad word with "happy" it would make a great nick.

Some month after I started playing I began constructing maps. Soon after that I opened my first server, HC // Construction. It ran until end of 2010 when it was closed due to lack of players. Less than two years later I opened HC // Fun.

That's my story.
